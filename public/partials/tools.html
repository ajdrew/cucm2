Choose the Right Stick

  • June 1, 2016
  • Tools

I belive in the right tool for the job. How you use tools and what tools you use says something about a person. Maybe you have a few that are prized posessions. Maybe you have a lot, but don't know how to use half of them. Maybe you have some, but only use them certain ways. See what I mean?

I use hardware, software, physical, and mental tools. We all do. Being a person who pursues quite a bit of productivity, I strive for a balence and to find the right fit.

Here are the criteria that I judge by.

  • Cross-platform?
  • Paper or app?
  • Stability or cutting edge?
  • Style or ruggedness?
  • Will it stand up to every day use?
  • Comfort or utility?
  • Budget, quality, or extravagant?
  • Need or want?

Here are some of the tools that work well for me:

Evernote

Huge utility in this app. While it may be difficult to understand at first because it is a blank canvas, this app is very definitely my second brain, document archive, RSS feed, todo list, interesting stuff archive, books list, notification center, project inspiration, etc.

Chrome

Google is a beast. For all the debate out there, it is a solid tool.

Spyderco Tenacious

The most solid utility/defence knife I have ever owned. The steel is superior with tines that can chew through anything.

Kimber Ultra CDP II Pro

It all depends on so much personally, but this is what does it for me.

Oakley

I where glasses and have kids. I need something that can stand the abuse and look stylish for at work. Oakley will hit the nail on the head every time.

Moleskine Volant Notebook

I'm still transitioning from paper to digital. This is the last vestig of paper that just works too well for jots to let it go.

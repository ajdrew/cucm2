Start Reading!

  • May 25, 2016
  • Books

Reading is a huge part of my life. I was a bookworm as a kid ... until college hit. In college, I got seriously burnt out on all the required reading and tech manuals. In 2012, my New Years resolution was to "Bring reading back as a joy and a means to improve intelligence, comprehension, and vocabulary. I would like to read 20 books before the end of the year." In 2015, I read 47 books!

However, I don't "read" any of my books. I listen to them! I can listen while mowning the lawn, yardwark, driving, etc. It is really hard for me to get serious reading time with kids when I sit down and almost instantly fall asleep.

I have had to find my groove when getting back into reading. If I start "reading" and am not gripped, it is time to jump to the next hot read to keep my motivation going. Most of my reading is now informational, historical, motivational, or productivity bent. It is rare when I can find a fictional book that can keep my interest. T

Below are my recommendations for "reading":

  • Hit up your local library first, instead of Amazon to save money. If the library doesn't have it, see if they will order it. By the time they get it, you will probably be ready for it.
  • Get an awesome Bluetooth headset. Suggestions: LG 700 or Plantronics M165
  • Turn off the radio and start listening to something worth while.

While it is difficult to not recommend more, here are my top recommendations:

Information Diet

Clay Johnson

This is a manual for millenials to manage communications habbits (that are most likely need changing). However, the final chanpters hold an immense vision for the future of technology that cannot be ignored.

The Lion of Sabray

Patrick Robinson

This book will reshape our uninclusive world view in a humbling, historical way. While Patrick Robinson is the aclaimed author of Lone Survivor, I would argue that this book better displays the humanity and 2,000 year religeous perspective of the person that helped save Marcus Luttrell.

Common Sense

Thomas Paine

Thomas Paine hit the social issues of the time square on the head. The fact that we are still dealing with the same issues, should be a sobering fact.

Masters of Deception

Michelle Slatalla

This is a fun idolization of early 1990 hacker culture. This illumines the allure of the Internet and the free information it promises.

Disrupted

Dan Lyons

This is a kick in the balls to millenials and our shallow views of business and companies. Dan Lyons does a masterful job of showing current business and personal strategies and behavior which will really get you to think deeper about your next job.

America: Imagine a World Without Her

Dinesh D'Souza

How do you sustain current economic, moral, and societal normalacy within the USA? Dinesh D'Souza deftly lays historical and current views contrasted against other cultures.
